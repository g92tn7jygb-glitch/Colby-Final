BRASIL 🇧🇷

Two years. One country. A lifetime of memories. This is my love letter to Brazil.

Why Brazil?

In 2022 I had the opportunity to spend two full years living in Brazil, completely immersed in the language, culture, and people. I wasn't a tourist — I lived there, worked there, and grew there.

Brazil is unlike anywhere else on Earth. It is a country of contradictions and wonders — massive cities and untouched rainforest, deep hardship and incredible joy. The people carry a warmth that is simply unmatched anywhere I've been.

The São Paulo skyline — where I spent most of my time in Brazil.

My Experience

I served as a service leader in South São Paulo, guiding a group of young people through community outreach projects across the region. It was challenging, meaningful, and life-changing work.

Top Lessons Brazil Taught Me

  1. Language opens every door.
    • I became fluent in Portuguese within six months through full immersion.
    • Speaking someone's language earns instant trust and respect.
    • I now think and dream in Portuguese sometimes.
  2. Community is everything.
    • Neighborhoods in São Paulo have a fierce, proud local identity.
    • People look out for one another in ways I had never seen before.
  3. Resilience is a way of life.
    • Many people I met faced real hardship daily yet never lost their spirit.
    • That perspective permanently changed how I handle my own challenges.
  4. Joy is always within reach.
    • Whether a neighborhood BBQ or a street soccer game, celebration was constant.
    • Brazilians don't wait for a reason to be happy — they create one.

The Food 🍖

Brazilian food is extraordinary. From the sizzle of a churrasco grill to a warm bowl of feijoada on a rainy afternoon — eating in Brazil is a full cultural experience.

Feijoada — Brazil's national dish. Black beans slow-cooked with pork.

Feijoada

The national dish. Black beans and pork slow-cooked together, served with rice, farofa, and orange slices.

Churrasco

Brazilian BBQ. Huge cuts of beef on skewers over open fire. At a rodízio they keep bringing it until you say stop.

Coxinha

Fried dough stuffed with shredded chicken. The perfect street food snack, found on every corner in São Paulo.

Açaí

Not the smoothie bowl you know. In Brazil it's thick, dark purple, and served with granola and banana. Incredible.
